ABOUT ME

  • Hi! I am a scientist, biker and an amateur baker.

    I studied at the University of Toronto, focussing on how mutations can rewire the genome and its impact on phenotypes. I am currently a scientist at Hexagon Bio, where I use functional genomics and pooled genetic screens to find new antibody-drug conjugates to target cancer. I’m part of an incredibly talented cross-disciplinary team tackling some of the most aggressive indications in oncology.

    When I'm not running experiments, you can find me biking in the local hills, tackling a classic segment or biking with Alto Velo. In my spare time, I like to find cool places to bike around the world and read books. I also like to think about how to build tools to help better model experiments in the lab, namely through vibe coding.

    Fun fact: I've lived in 4 of the top 20 most expensive cities in the world so far.
